Background

Platform leadership for global asset management.

I set the product strategy and governance model for Spectrum's core platform at J.P.Morgan Asset Management: the shared foundation that investment, client service, and operations teams build on across 100+ applications. The work is about coherence at scale: making deliberate decisions about what belongs in the platform, on what terms teams adopt it, and how emerging capabilities like AI and advanced tooling are absorbed without fragmenting the estate.

The core strategic bet behind Spectrum's platform model is that consistency at scale reduces cost and risk more than team autonomy does. When 100+ applications each solve foundational problems independently: design, analytics, support, AI tooling. The quality diverges, the duplication compounds, and the user experience fragments. A governed platform model prevents that, but only if the shared products are genuinely better than what teams would build themselves, and only if adoption is structured rather than optional.

My work is holding that model together: setting the roadmap and governance across the shared product portfolio, influencing product teams across the estate to adopt strategic core capabilities, and making the calls (what to invest in, what to defer, what to govern versus leave to teams) that keep the platform coherent as it grows. The technical and financial depth (CFA, MSc CS) matters here because the conversations that shape platform direction happen with engineering leads, business stakeholders, and compliance teams, not just product teams.
