## Problem
Spectrum needed shared visibility products that could support investment, client service, and operations users consistently across the platform. Without a strategic dashboards capability, teams risked building local solutions that delivered inconsistent workflow quality and did not scale.

Dashboards had to work as a core platform product — serving many user groups with a shared, governed capability — not as isolated reporting surfaces owned by individual teams.

## Approach
I included Dashboards as part of the Spectrum core product roadmap and led it as a shared service — applying the same product and governance model used across the platform. That meant aligning product direction with the needs of multiple user groups, connecting delivery to shared tooling, and ensuring the capability contributed to platform-wide consistency rather than adding to fragmentation.

## Solution
Dashboards became one of the strategic products helping users across Spectrum navigate portfolio information more effectively. It gave the platform a shared, governed visibility surface — supporting more consistent workflows across investment, client service, and operations rather than requiring teams to rebuild locally.

Like the other core products I own, Dashboards was developed through close triad partnership with Engineering and Design, ensuring resilience, enterprise-grade quality, and a consistent user experience.

### Product decisions
- Led Dashboards as a shared platform product with a clear roadmap, not as a team-level reporting feature
- Aligned delivery with platform governance so the capability scaled consistently across user groups
- Used triad partnership with Engineering and Design to maintain quality and resilience at enterprise scale

## Impact
- Supported thousands of internal users across investment, client service, and operations
- Delivered more consistent portfolio workflow visibility across the Spectrum estate
- Strengthened the set of strategic shared products underpinning the platform
