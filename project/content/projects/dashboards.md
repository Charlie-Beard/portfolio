## Problem
Spectrum needed shared visibility products that could support investment, client service, and operations users in a consistent way. Without a strategic dashboards capability, teams risked inconsistent workflow quality and local solutions that did not scale well across the platform.

Dashboards had to work as a core platform product, not just as isolated reporting surfaces for individual teams.

## Approach
I included Dashboards as part of the Spectrum core product roadmap and treated it as a shared service supporting broader platform adoption and quality. That meant aligning product direction with governance, shared tooling, and the needs of multiple user groups.

## Solution
Dashboards became one of the strategic products helping users navigate Spectrum more effectively. It supported more consistent portfolio workflows and gave the platform another shared surface teams could rely on rather than rebuild.

The product also benefited from the same triad partnership model with Engineering and Design, helping ensure resilience and user focus at enterprise scale.

## Impact
- Supported thousands of internal users across investment, client service, and operations
- Contributed to more consistent portfolio workflows in Spectrum
- Strengthened the set of shared core products used across the wider platform
