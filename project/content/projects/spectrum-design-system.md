## Problem
Spectrum supports thousands of internal users across investment, client service, and operations. As the platform grew, maintaining a consistent, high-quality user experience across a 100+ application estate became a platform problem rather than a local design problem.

Without a strategic shared design foundation, product teams risked duplicating patterns, diverging in workflow quality, and slowing delivery across the broader platform.

## Approach
I owned roadmap, prioritisation, OKRs, and delivery for core platform capabilities, including the design system. The job was not only to define components and patterns, but to make the design system a product that teams across Spectrum would actively adopt.

That meant influencing product teams across the platform to use strategic core products, tooling, and processes, and partnering closely with Engineering and Design leads as a triad.

## Solution
Spectrum Design System became one of the core products underpinning the platform. It provided a shared product and design foundation teams could use to create more consistent portfolio workflows while reducing reinvention across applications.

The product also served a governance role. It gave teams a strategic path for building within Spectrum so the overall experience stayed coherent as the platform expanded.

### Product decisions
- Treated the design system as a strategic core product rather than a passive library
- Used triad partnership with Engineering and Design to keep quality and resilience aligned
- Positioned adoption and consistency as platform-level outcomes, not just team-level preferences

## Impact
- Supported a consistently high-quality user experience across Spectrum
- Contributed to consistent portfolio workflows used by thousands of internal users
- Helped accelerate product delivery across a 100+ application platform estate

This work mattered because it gave Spectrum a durable shared foundation as new products and workflows continued to scale.
